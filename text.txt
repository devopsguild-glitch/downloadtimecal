<p>
Internet speeds have evolved dramatically over time, from early dial-up connections
measured in kilobits per second to modern fiber and 5G connections capable of gigabit
speeds. Because download speeds are typically measured in <strong>bits per second (bps)</strong>
while file sizes are measured in <strong>bytes</strong>, converting between them requires
remembering that <strong>1 byte = 8 bits</strong>. The table below shows approximate
average speeds for different connection technologies and the estimated time it would
take to download a <strong>1 GB file</strong> using each type of connection.
</p>
